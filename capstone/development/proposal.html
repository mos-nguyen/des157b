Capstone Proposal

Topic: Censorship

My idea for the Capstone Project will about what would happen if censorship went too far, not allowing for anything that could be considered offensive to see the light of day. The project will follow the narrative of a detective/private investigator who is trying to solve a crime, but is held back by censorship. Clues, witnesses, and evidence will be limited, depending on how much the detective is willing to risk breaking censorship.

Identified Lenses

Speculative Design

  • What if censorship was controlled all by one person, or a small group of people?
  • What if we stopped censoring everything all at once? How would people react?
  • What if, instead of censoring, those topics were shared and discussed. Would people learn from history? Or would people not care and continue on like normal.

Mental Models

  • The idea that censorship helps protect children
  • Censorship prevents people from wanting to partake in offensive language and actions

Utopian/Dystopian Narratives

A dystopian future where censorship has gone past just certain words or topics, affecting the way people think and act as well. Freedom and self expression is taken away from society, controlled by whoever is in charge of what is considered "safe". It would eventually lead to everyone becoming the same, or people living in fear of breaking censorship.

Potential Features

  • A "health bar" indicating how much the detective is catering to the censorship laws
  • An inventory for the detective to collect clues and evidence
  • A way to examine and combine evidence to piece together what happened
  • Text boxes for character dialoge and story narration

Possible Challenges/Concers

There may be some people who do not want to censor themselves, but are required to due to the rules of the app/media they use. In this case, my capstone project may not be as effective in changing how people view censorship.

The difference between censorship and safe spaces may be difficult to acknowledge in my capstone project. I'm not sure if this is something that should be included in my project, but it could be argued that safe spaces are a way to censor. I don't want to discourage safe spaces, but the nature of my topic could very easily bleed into that idea.

Without censorship, would people be encourage to partake in offensive language and actions? Some examples of why censorship is needed are swear words/slurs, sexual content, and disturbing content. Censorship can help prevent the spread of these topics, but I believe that it's not a permanant solution. It would be impossible to censor everything everywhere, so what would happen if someone just saw these topics elsewhere?

Target Audience

I want to be able to reach people who are affected by censorship, either by having to self censor, or having content be censored. Younger people are more likely to encounter censorship, especially online. My target audience will be people aged highschool to college/young adult since they are most likely to be the age group who spends the most time online, and therefore has experienced censorship of some kind. People who consume and create posts online are the ideal target audience for my Capstone project since they're more likely to directly experience seeing or being censored.
